History of the Debre Zeyit Berhane Wongel Baptist Church

The church started in Debre Zeyit in Berhane’s house with just 5 people as a cell in 1995 and then it continued increasing until it came to 17 in 1996. Because the house in which we were worshipping was not sufficient, we rented a house for our service in the year 2000.  The number of believers rose to 50.

Surprisingly enough we had won 25 souls to Christ from a place called “Denkaka” which is 10 kilometers away from the main church in Debre Zeyit from 1996 to 2000.

The current figure of church members is 200 in Debre Zeyit central church and in the outreach we have 50 in number.

Fund raising was held at this level and the lord assisted us to buy the compound in which we are serving our lord at this moment we are worshipping our Lord in a plastic shelter.

Church leaders and all the other servants of the Lord in our church are strongly praying before the lord so that he enables us to construct church building and other necessary offices for the church as a leading condition. In the second level our need targets on establishing Bible school so that the servants of God in our church get training so that they are up graded to serve the Lord according to their vocation. While our heritage is Baptist our core beliefs are the foundation of Christian faith.

Debre Zeyit town has its own history.  Idol worship and pagan beliefs are wide spread.  Even in the modern times, it is no surprise to see people flow to the “Erecha” festival of pagan worship.  It has its popularity even among government circles. The city of Debre Zeyit is extended along 34.8 square kilometers, largely surrounded by crater lakes, some of which serve as cradle of this pagan worship.

One can easily understand what dearly a price a born again Christian has to pay in this area to keep up his Christian faith and principles.
